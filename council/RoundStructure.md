# Round Structure

## Quick Council

Use when speed matters more than transcript richness.

### Round 1 - Initial Positions
Each persona answers:
- what is your recommendation?
- what is the biggest risk?
- what are others likely underweighting?

Then synthesize immediately.

## Full Council

Use for higher-stakes decisions.

### Round 1 - Initial Positions
Each persona gives:
- recommendation
- reasoning from their lens
- top risks
- assumptions they think others may miss

Goal:
- surface distinct viewpoints before interaction starts

### Round 2 - Responses & Challenges
Each persona reads Round 1 and must:
- reference at least 1-2 specific personas
- challenge assumptions or logic
- refine their own stance if needed

Good examples:
- “I disagree with Reliability Engineer’s assumption that rollback is cheap because…”
- “Building on the Buyer/CFO lens, the current framing still doesn’t justify cost because…”

Goal:
- generate real intellectual friction

### Round 3 - Final Position / Convergence
Each persona gives:
- final recommendation
- where they now agree with the council
- where they still disagree
- what they would do next if forced to choose today

Goal:
- expose convergence without flattening genuine disagreement

## Synthesis Requirements

After the final round, the parent agent must synthesize:
- areas of convergence
- unresolved disagreements
- recommended path
- key tradeoffs
- open questions
- next step

## Timing Guidance

- Quick council: usually 15-45 seconds total
- Full council: usually 30-120 seconds total

## Quality Bar

Bad council:
- five bland takes
- no cross-reference
- everyone agrees because the prompts were vague

Good council:
- specific tradeoffs
- direct challenge
- at least one persona changes or sharpens stance after debate
- synthesis feels more useful than the average individual answer
